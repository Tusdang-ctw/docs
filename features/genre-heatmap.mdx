The Genre Heatmap provides a bird's-eye view of the entire Roblox genre landscape. See which genres are oversaturated, which are growing fast, and where the real opportunities are — all in one visual dashboard.

## Why Genre Selection Matters

Choosing the wrong genre is the #1 mistake new Roblox developers make. Building a simulator when the market already has 8,500 of them means you're competing with established titles for a finite pool of players. But building in an underserved genre with growing demand? That's how breakout games happen.

The Genre Heatmap turns this decision from guesswork into data.

## How It Works

The **Genre Opportunity Engine** analyzes every genre across five dimensions:

| Dimension | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Game count | How many active games exist in this genre | Competition level |
| Average CCU | How many players the genre attracts | Market demand |
| Growth rate | Week-over-week change in genre CCU | Momentum |
| Revenue potential | Average gamepass revenue per game | Monetization opportunity |
| Saturation score | Ratio of games to players | How crowded the genre is |

These combine into a single **opportunity score** (0–1) for each genre.

## Reading the Heatmap

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="High score (0.7–1.0) — Green zone">
    Strong demand, low competition. These genres have growing player bases but relatively few games competing. Ideal for new developers or studios looking to enter a fresh market.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Medium score (0.4–0.7) — Yellow zone">
    Moderate opportunity. Either the genre is growing but competitive, or it's less competitive but also lower demand. Can still be profitable with strong execution and differentiation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Low score (0–0.4) — Red zone">
    Oversaturated or declining. Too many games fighting for too few players, or the genre is shrinking. Not impossible to succeed, but you'll need a genuinely unique angle.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Use Cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="First-time developer choosing a genre">
    Open the heatmap and sort by opportunity score. Pick a green-zone genre that matches your skills. You'll have a much better chance of getting noticed than jumping into a saturated market.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Studio diversifying its portfolio">
    If all your games are simulators, check the heatmap for adjacent genres with higher opportunity scores. Expanding into a growing genre reduces your portfolio risk.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Validating a game concept">
    Before spending weeks building, check if your target genre is green or red. A red-zone genre doesn't mean "don't build" — it means you need a stronger unique selling point.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips

<Tip>
  The highest opportunity isn't always the best choice for *you*. A genre with a 0.6 score that matches your skills will outperform a 0.9 genre you have no experience with. Use the heatmap as one input, not the only input.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Check the heatmap monthly. Genre dynamics shift — a green-zone genre can become yellow within weeks if multiple developers jump in simultaneously.
</Tip>
