Launch Timing helps you pick the optimal release window for your Roblox game. Launching at the right time can mean 2-3x more players in your critical first week — when Roblox's algorithm is deciding whether to boost your game.

## Why Launch Timing Matters

Roblox's discovery algorithm heavily weights a game's early performance. If your game gets strong CCU in its first 48 hours, it's more likely to appear on the Discover page, which creates a virtuous cycle of more players, more visibility, and more players.

Launching during the wrong week — say, right when a massive seasonal event ends and players leave the platform — can doom an otherwise great game to obscurity.

## How It Works

The engine combines three data sources to generate recommendations:

1. **Seasonal event calendar** — 9 major Roblox events tracked (Halloween, Winter Holiday, Summer Break, Spring Break, Easter, Back to School, Thanksgiving, Valentine's Day, and Anniversary events)
2. **Historical CCU patterns** — genre-specific player activity data showing when each genre peaks and dips throughout the year
3. **Domain knowledge** — rules about player behavior (e.g., younger players are more active during school breaks, horror games spike around Halloween)

## Seasonal Events

| Event | Peak Period | Best For |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Summer Break | Jun 1 – Aug 15 | All genres (highest overall CCU) |
| Halloween | Oct 1 – Oct 31 | Horror, Spooky, Mystery |
| Winter Holiday | Dec 15 – Jan 5 | All genres (gift card spending spike) |
| Spring Break | Mar 10 – Mar 25 | Social, Roleplay, Casual |
| Back to School | Aug 20 – Sep 10 | Avoid launching (CCU drops) |

## Use Cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Horror game developer">
    Launch timing will recommend October 1-15 as your optimal window — early enough to catch the Halloween hype wave but before the market gets saturated with seasonal horror games. Avoid November entirely.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Simulator developer">
    Simulators perform consistently year-round but peak during school breaks. Launch Timing would recommend early June (Summer Break start) or late December (Winter Holiday + gift card influx).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Planning a major update">
    Not just for launches — use timing data to schedule your biggest content updates during peak periods for your genre. A major update during a CCU peak gets more eyeballs and better retention.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Output

For each game theme/genre, you receive:
- **Ranked launch windows** with opportunity scores (0–1)
- **Events to target** with explanations (e.g., "Horror CCU peaks 200-300% during Halloween season")
- **Windows to avoid** with reasons (e.g., "Back to School week — platform CCU drops 25%")
- **Genre-specific patterns** showing weekly and monthly player behavior

## Tips

<Tip>
  Don't just launch during peak CCU — launch 1-2 weeks *before* the peak. This gives your game time to build momentum and get picked up by the algorithm right as player numbers surge.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Combine Launch Timing with Genre Heatmap data. If your genre is low-competition AND you time the launch perfectly, you'll have the maximum chance of breaking out.
</Tip>
