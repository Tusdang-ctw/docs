Alerts notify you when important events happen to your tracked games — CCU spikes, trend changes, or competitor movements. Never miss a critical moment again.

## Creating an Alert

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Alerts">
    Open the **Alerts** page from the sidebar navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Alert">
    Click the **New Alert** button to open the alert creation form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the alert">
    Select the tracked game, choose an alert type, and set your threshold value. For example: "Alert me when Blox Fruits CCU exceeds 500,000."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click Save. The alert is now active and will be checked every 15 minutes by the background worker.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Alert Types

| Type | What It Does | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| CCU Spike | Triggers when a game's CCU exceeds your threshold | "Notify me when my game hits 10,000 CCU" |
| Trend Change | Triggers when a game's trend direction reverses | "Alert me if Blox Fruits starts declining" |
| Competitor | Triggers when a competing game in your genre makes a big move | "Watch for any RPG game that jumps 50% CCU" |

## When to Use Each Alert Type

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="CCU Spike — milestone tracking">
    Set CCU alerts at key milestones for your own games: 1,000 CCU, 5,000 CCU, 10,000 CCU. Each milestone means different things for discoverability and revenue. Also useful for competitor monitoring — "alert me if Game X breaks 100K."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Trend Change — catching reversals">
    The most strategic alert type. If a competitor that's been growing steadily suddenly starts declining, that's a window of opportunity. Conversely, if your own game's trend reverses downward, you want to know immediately — not a week later.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Competitor — genre awareness">
    Cast a wider net. Instead of tracking individual games, monitor an entire genre for significant movements. Great for staying aware of new entrants that could disrupt your market.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Managing Alerts

- **View all alerts**: Visit the Alerts page or call `GET /api/alerts/my`
- **Mark as read**: Click an alert to mark it as viewed
- **Delete**: Remove alerts you no longer need to reduce noise
- **Review history**: See past triggered alerts to understand patterns

## Email Alerts

Email notifications are available on **Builder** and **Studio** plans. When enabled, you receive an email in addition to the in-dashboard notification — so you're informed even when you're not logged in.

<Note>
  Alerts are processed every 15 minutes by the background Celery worker. This means there can be up to a 15-minute delay between a threshold being crossed and the alert firing.
</Note>

## Tips

<Tip>
  Start with fewer, high-value alerts. 3 well-configured alerts are better than 20 noisy ones. Focus on thresholds that actually require action from you.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Set CCU alerts at *actionable* thresholds, not vanity milestones. "Alert me at 5,000 CCU so I can enable the monetization update" is more useful than "alert me at every 1,000 increment."
</Tip>

## Via API

```bash
# Create an alert
curl -X POST https://rolearn.dev/api/alerts \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"alert_type": "ccu_spike", "universe_id": 2753915549, "threshold": 500000}'

# Get your alerts
curl https://rolearn.dev/api/alerts/my \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN"
```

See [API Reference → Create Alert](/api-reference/create-alert) and [Get Alerts](/api-reference/get-alerts) for full details.
