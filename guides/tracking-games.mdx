Tracking a game adds it to your personal watchlist. Tracked games get priority CCU monitoring, forecast generation, alert capabilities, and appear in your War Room dashboard.

## How to Track a Game

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a game">
    Use the **search bar** at the top of any page, or browse the Trending, Popular, or Genre pages. You can also enter a Roblox Place ID directly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Track">
    On the game detail page, click the **Track** button. The game is instantly added to your watchlist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your tracked games">
    All tracked games appear on your dashboard and in the War Room. You'll start seeing CCU history build up immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up alerts (optional)">
    For any tracked game, create alerts to be notified of CCU spikes, trend changes, or competitor movements. See [Setting Alerts](/guides/setting-alerts) for details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Tracking Enables

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="CCU History" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    See detailed CCU charts for the past 7–90 days depending on your plan. Data is captured every 5 minutes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Forecasts" icon="chart-line">
    Prophet-based CCU predictions become available once a game has enough historical data (best with 7+ days).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Alerts" icon="bell">
    Set up notifications for CCU thresholds, trend reversals, or competitor movements on any tracked game.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Benchmarking" icon="scale-balanced">
    Quick-access benchmarking against genre competitors — see where your tracked games rank.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Update Tracking" icon="arrow-trend-up">
    Log game updates and measure their CCU impact with before/after analysis (Builder+ plans).
  </Card>
  <Card title="War Room" icon="display">
    All tracked games appear in the War Room for real-time unified monitoring (Studio plan).
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What to Track

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Your own games">
    The most obvious use case. Track all your published games to monitor CCU trends, get forecasts, and measure the impact of your updates.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Direct competitors">
    Track 3-5 games in your genre that compete for the same audience. When their CCU drops, it might be an opportunity for you. When it spikes, investigate what they did.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Genre leaders">
    Track the top games in genres you're interested in. Their CCU patterns reveal genre-level trends — seasonal peaks, weekly patterns, and growth trajectories.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Emerging games">
    Spot a game on the Breakout Radar? Track it to watch its trajectory. Early-stage games are the most interesting to follow because their growth patterns are still forming.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Untracking a Game

To remove a game from your watchlist, go to the game detail page and click **Untrack**. Historical data is retained — if you re-track the game later, your previous CCU history will still be available.

## Tracking Limits

| Plan | Max Tracked Games |
|------|:-:|
| Explorer | 3 |
| Builder | 15 |
| Studio | 50 |

<Tip>
  Prioritize tracking games you actively check. 3 well-chosen tracked games on Explorer is more valuable than 50 random games on Studio. Focus on your own games + your top 2-3 competitors.
</Tip>

## Via API

```bash
# Track a game
curl -X POST https://rolearn.dev/api/track/2753915549 \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN"

# Untrack a game
curl -X DELETE https://rolearn.dev/api/track/2753915549 \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN"
```

See [API Reference → Track Game](/api-reference/track-game) and [Untrack Game](/api-reference/untrack-game) for full details.
